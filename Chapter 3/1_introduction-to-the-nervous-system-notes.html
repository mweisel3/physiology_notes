Introduction to the Nervous System

▶What is the primary function of the nervous system?
  • The nervous system is a communication and control network that allows an organism to react and adapt to its internal and external environment.
  • It operates through a three-step process:
    1. Sensory Input: It gathers information from the environment using specialized sensors called receptors.
    2. Integration: The nervous system processes this information, combining it with memories and goals to decide on a course of action.
    3. Motor Output: It issues commands to effector organs, like muscles and glands, to carry out the desired response.
▶What are the two main divisions of the nervous system?
  • Central Nervous System (CNS): This division consists of the brain and the spinal cord.
  • Peripheral Nervous System (PNS): This division consists of the nerves and ganglia (clusters of neurons) located outside of the skull and spinal column. It serves as the interface between the environment and the CNS.
▶How is the Peripheral Nervous System (PNS) functionally divided?
  • The PNS has two major functional pathways:
    • Sensory (Afferent) Division: Carries sensory information from the body's receptors to the CNS.
    • Motor (Efferent) Division: Carries commands from the CNS out to the body's effector organs.
▶How is the Motor (Efferent) Division subdivided?
  • The motor division is split into two systems:
    • Somatic Nervous System (SNS): Primarily controls voluntary actions, such as the movement of skeletal muscles.
    • Autonomic Nervous System (ANS): Regulates involuntary bodily functions and is further divided into the sympathetic and parasympathetic branches.
▶What are the two main types of cells in the nervous system?
  • Neurons: These are the primary signaling cells. Their function is to generate and transmit signals to other neurons or to effector organs. The point of communication between neurons is called a synapse.
  • Glia (or Neuroglia): These are support cells. They sustain neurons both metabolically and physically and play an important role in shaping the activity of the nervous system.

Summary

The nervous system functions by receiving sensory input, integrating it, and producing a motor output. It is structurally divided into the Central Nervous System (CNS) and the Peripheral Nervous System (PNS). The PNS is further split into sensory (afferent) and motor (efferent) divisions. The two main cell types that make up the system are the signaling neurons and the supportive glia.
