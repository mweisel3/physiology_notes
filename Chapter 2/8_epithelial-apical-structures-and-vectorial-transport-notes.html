Epethilial Apical STructures and Vectorial Transport

▶What are the three main specializations of the apical surface?
  • The apical surface of epithelial cells can have several specialized structures to aid its function. The three main types are:
    1. Microvilli
    2. Stereocilia
    3. Cilia
▶What are microvilli and stereocilia?
  • Microvilli and stereocilia are non-motile, finger-like projections of the apical membrane.
  • Their primary function is to increase the surface area of the cell, which is crucial for cells that need to transport large quantities of substances, like those in the small intestine.
  • Stereocilia are structurally similar to microvilli but are much longer.
▶What are the two types of cilia and their functions?
  • Primary Cilia: These are non-motile and typically, there is only one per cell. They function as mechanoreceptors, sensing the rate of fluid flow, such as in the kidney nephrons.
  • Secondary Cilia: These are motile. Their function is to move substances along the epithelial surface. A key example is the coordinated beating of cilia in the respiratory tract to clear mucus.
▶What is vectorial transport and what makes it possible?
  • Vectorial transport is the directional movement of a substance from one side of an epithelial sheet to the opposite side.
  • This process is made possible by two key features of epithelial cells:
    1. Tight junctions act as a "fence," dividing the plasma membrane into distinct apical and basolateral domains.
    2. Specific membrane transport proteins are segregated into one domain or the other. This forces substances to follow a specific path through the cell, enabling directional transport.

Summary

The apical surface of epithelial cells has specialized structures: microvilli and stereocilia increase surface area for absorption, while cilia can be motile (to move substances) or non-motile (to sense flow). This structural specialization, combined with the cell polarity created by tight junctions, allows epithelial cells to perform directional vectorial transport across the entire tissue.
