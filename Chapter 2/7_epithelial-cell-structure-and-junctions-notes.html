Epithelial Cell Structure and Junctions

▶What are epithelial cells and what is their key structural feature?
  • Epithelial cells are arranged in sheets and form the interface between the body's internal environment and the external world.
  • They have many functions, including forming a barrier against microbes, preventing water loss, and maintaining homeostasis.
  • Their key feature is polarity. They have two distinct sides:
    • The apical side faces the external environment or the lumen (the inside of a tube or organ).
    • The basal side faces the interior of the body and rests on a layer called the basal lamina.
▶What is the function of a Desmosome?
  • Desmosomes are anchoring junctions that act like "spot welds" to provide strong mechanical adhesion between adjacent cells.
  • They connect the intermediate filaments of the cytoskeleton from one cell to the next.
▶What is the function of a Gap Junction?
  • Gap junctions are communicating junctions that form a direct channel between the cytoplasm of two neighboring cells.
  • These channels allow for the rapid passage of ions and small molecules, effectively coupling the cells electrically and metabolically.
▶What are the two main functions of a Tight Junction?
  • A tight junction has two critical roles:
    1. "Fence" Function: It separates the plasma membrane into distinct apical and basolateral domains, preventing membrane proteins from moving between them. This is essential for creating cell polarity and enabling directional transport.
    2. "Gate" Function: It forms a seal between cells that regulates the paracellular pathway, which is the path for the movement of water, ions, and small molecules between the cells.
▶What is the function of a Hemidesmosome?
  • Hemidesmosomes are anchoring junctions that connect the epithelial cell's cytoskeleton to the underlying basal lamina and connective tissue.
▶What are the two pathways for transport across an epithelium?
  1. Transcellular Pathway: Movement of a substance through the cell, which requires it to cross both the apical and the basolateral membranes.
  2. Paracellular Pathway: Movement of a substance between the cells, passing through the tight junctions.

Summary

Epithelial cells are polarized sheets that form the body's barriers. They are held together by various junctions: desmosomes and hemidesmosomes provide anchoring, gap junctions allow for communication, and tight junctions create the cell's polarity and regulate the paracellular pathway. Substances cross the epithelium either through the cells (transcellular) or between them (paracellular).
