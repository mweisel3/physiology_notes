Bulk Transport and Clinical Applications

▶What is Cystic Fibrosis(CF)?
  • CF is a severe hereditary disease characterized by chronic lung infections and pancreatic insufficiency
  • It is caused by mutations in a gene that codes for an ABC transporter (specifically, the CFTR protein)
  • The most common mutation causes the transporter protein to be degraded before it reaches the plasma membrane
▶How does ABC transporter defect cause disease?
  • In healthy airways, a thin layer of mucus traps bacteria and particles, which are then cleared by cilia (the mucociliary escalator) .
  • In CF, the defective transporter disrupts ion and water secretion, causing the airway mucus to become thick and sticky
  • This prevents the clearance of bacteria, leading to chronic infections, lung damage, and ultimately respiratory failure
▶What is vesicular transport?

Moving large quantities of solutes and water in/out of the cell by wrapping them in a piece of membrane

▶What is the difference between endocytosis and exocytosis?
  • Endocytosis is the process of bringing substances into the cell by pinching off a piece of the cell's membrane to form a vesicle
  • Exocysotit - is exiting substances from a cell where the vesicle that carries the substance integrates into the cell membrane
▶What is transcytosis

Bringing a substance across the entire cell via a vesicle (it is the combination of endocytosis and exocytosis)

▶What are the 3 kinds of endocytosis?

Pinocytosis: "Cell drinking"; the nonspecific uptake of small molecules and water into the cell Phagocytosis: "Cell eating"; the internalization of large particles like bacteria or cell debris. This is a key feature of immune cells Receptor-mediated Endocytosis: The specific uptake of molecules that first bind to a receptor on the cell surface

Summary

Defects in membrane transporters can lead to serious diseases like Cystic Fibrosis, where faulty ion transport impairs lung function. In addition to transport via channels and carriers, cells move bulk materials using vesicular transport. Endocytosis brings materials in through pinocytosis, phagocytosis, or receptor-mediated processes. Exocytosis releases materials out. Transcytosis moves materials across the entire cell.
