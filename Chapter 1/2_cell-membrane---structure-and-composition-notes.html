Cell Membrane - Structure and Composition

▶ What is the plasma membrane?
  • The plasma membrane is a barrier that surrounds all cells, separating the intracellular contents from the extracellular environment .
  • It is a 5-nm-thick lipid bilayer with associated proteins .
  • The structure consists of an inner and outer leaflet.
▶ What are the key functions of the membrane?
  • Selective Transport: Regulates the passage of molecules into and out of the cell
  • Cell Communication: Involves receptors for hormones and neurotransmitters
  • Cell Recognition: Uses cell surface antigens
  • Tissue Organization: Forms junctions between cells
  • Enzymatic Activity: Some membrane proteins are enzymes
  • Determining Cell Shape: Links the cytoskeleton to the membrane.
▶ What is an amphipathic molecule?
  • A molecule has both a hydrophilic ("water-loving") region and a hydrophobic ("water-fearing") region .
  • Other terms include hydrophilic (dissolves in water, e.g., salt, sugar) and hydrophobic (repels water, e.g., oil).
▶ What are the main components of the membrane?
  • Phospholipids
  • Proteins
  • Phosphogycerides
▶ What are phospholipids?
  • Phospholipids are the main lipids that make up the cell membrane .
  • They are amphipathic molecules.
▶ Describe the structure of a phospholipid.
  • They contain a charged, hydrophilic "head" and two nonpolar, hydrophobic fatty acyl "tails"
  • Most have a glycerol "backbone"
  • Two fatty acid chains and a polar head group (often an alcohol connected via a phosphate group) are attached to the glycerol
▶ How do phospholipids form the membrane?
  • The amphipathic nature is critical for forming the bilayer
  • The hydrophobic tails face inward, forming the core of the membrane, away from water
  • The hydrophilic heads are exposed to the aqueous environment on the inner and outer surfaces of the cell

Summary

The plasma membrane is a selective barrier composed of a lipid bilayer and proteins that performs many crucial functions, including transport and communication. Its fundamental structure is based on phospholipids, which are amphipathic molecules. Their hydrophilic heads face the surrounding water, while their hydrophobic tails form a nonpolar core, creating the distinct bilayer structure.
