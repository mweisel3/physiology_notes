Membrane Proteins

▶ How much of the membrane is protein?

Proteins are key to cell function and can be up to ~50% of the membrane

▶ What are the 3 main classes of membrane proteins?
Type Function Number of leaflets?
Integral (אינטגרלים) Embedded within the lipid bilayer 2
Peripheral (היקפיים) Loosely attached to surface 1
Lipid Anchored (מעוגנים) Attached to membrane via lipid molecule 1
▶ What are integral proteins?
  • They are embedded directly within the hydrophobic core of the lipid bilayer
  • Many are transmembrane proteins, which means they span the entire membrane from one side to the other
  • The regions of the protein that are within the membrane are hydrophobic, while the regions exposed to the watery environment on either side are hydrophilic.
▶ What are some functions of integral proteins?
  • Signal transduction
  • Cell adhesion to extra-cellular matrix
  • Form channels and transporters
▶ What are peripheral proteins?
  • Peripheral proteins are not embedded in the membrane's core. They usually bind to integral proteins or lipid-anchored proteins
  • They are involved in cell-to-cell communication and forming connections between cells
▶ What are lipid-anchored proteins?

These proteins are attached to the membrane by a strong, covalent bond to a lipid molecule, which is then embedded in one of the leaflets

Summary

Membrane proteins constitute roughly half the mass of the plasma membrane and are vital for its function. They are classified into three types: integral proteins, which are embedded in or span the bilayer; peripheral proteins, which are attached to the surface; and lipid-anchored proteins, which are covalently bonded to lipids. These proteins are crucial for cell transport, signaling, and structural support.
