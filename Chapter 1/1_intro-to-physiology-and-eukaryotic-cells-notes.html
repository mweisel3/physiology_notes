Intro to Physiology and Eukaryotic Cells

▶ What is Physiology?
  • Physiology is the science that studies the mechanical, physical, and biochemical functions of systems in living organisms .
  • Understanding normal function at both the cellular and systemic level is essential for understanding dysfunctions.
▶ What do cells need to survive?
  • The most fundamental condition for the body to function is cell survival .
  • Cell survival requires a supply of energy, maintenance of stable internal conditions, and protection from the hostile external environment.
▶ What are the main features of a Eukaryotic cell?
  • The key characteristic is the presence of a membrane-bound nucleus .
  • The cell is divided into two main compartments: the nucleus and the cytoplasm.
▶ What is the cytoplasm?
  • An aqueous solution containing ions, organic molecules, cytoskeletal elements, and organelles .
  • Most organelles are enclosed in their own membrane and perform specific cellular functions.

Summary

Physiology studies the function of living organisms, starting with the cell. For the body to work, cells must first survive by getting energy and maintaining stability. Eukaryotic cells are defined by a membrane-bound nucleus and contain various organelles like the mitochondria (for energy) and nucleus (for genetic material), each with a specific, vital function. The cell's structure is directly related to its role within a tissue or organ.
